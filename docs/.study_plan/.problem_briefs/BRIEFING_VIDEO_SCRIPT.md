# QuickBite Briefing Video — Script

A ~3–4 minute video voiced by **Mollie**, the QuickBite delivery manager. Played at the **Task briefing** step (`STUDY_DETAILED_PLAN.md` §5, step 5). Followed by participant reading the printed reference document (`PHYSICAL_REFERENCE.md`).

## Editor note (not for participants)

This script deliberately avoids optimization vocabulary (no "constraints", "soft / hard", "objectives", "trade-offs", "stochastic", "violations") and does **not** enumerate the cost-function components. Mollie speaks as a dispatcher — operational concerns, fleet anecdotes, driver personalities — leaving the participant to translate her concerns into goals, priorities, and rules in their own language when speaking with the AI. See `STUDY_DETAILED_PLAN.md` §1 (*Observe operational-to-formal translation*) and §5 step 5 for why this matters.

The script is also written for **non-native English speakers**: contractions are kept (they are universal in English audio) but heavy idioms are avoided (no *"pain in the neck"*, *"the whole bit"*, *"holler at"*, *"watching the clock"*, *"compare notes"*, etc.). Mollie still sounds informal, just not slang-heavy.

**Future edits:** if you find yourself (a) adding language like "we want to balance workload" or "this is a soft preference", soften it back into operational/anecdotal phrasing — that translation is what we're studying; (b) reaching for a colorful idiom, prefer the plain alternative — Mollie's character survives without slang.

## TTS conventions

- `[inhales]`, `[sniffles]`, `[clears throat]`, `[loud click]` etc. — voice ticks
- `[pause]`, `[long pause]` — in-line timing
- `<break time='Ns'/>` — SSML break between paragraphs
- `[slowly]`, `[firmly]`, `[low]`, `[energetically]`, `[lightly]`, `[whispering]` — delivery direction
- `### Scene N` markdown headers below are **for the recorder / director only** and are not narrated.

## Voice direction

A 35-year-old midwestern blue-collar woman with a low-to-medium-pitched voice. She sits next to a food delivery loading zone. Her voice is firm and relatively slow but a bit rushed; speech pattern is unstable. Audio quality similar to a smartphone voice recorder.

---

### Scene 1 — Hello, I'm Mollie

[loud click] [moving phone] [sniffle] ... [lightly]

[inhales] Hey, I'm Mollie. [energetically] I manage the fleet here at QuickBite. [sniffles] ... [slowly] They asked me to give you a quick overview of our situation, so you can take it from there with the AI tool. [pause] We've also printed the data [firmly] — orders, drivers, all of it — so you have it on paper too.



### Scene 2 — Tomorrow's setup

[inhales] So, for tomorrow [pause]. Thirty orders, five drivers. [pause] Orders are across five zones — Riverside, Harbor, Uptown, Westgate, and Northgate. [pause] Most drivers leave from the central depot in the morning. ..... Eve is the exception — her truck is parked in Northgate, loaded the night before [pause], so she starts from there.



### Scene 3 — Time windows and express

[inhales] The orders all have time windows — be there between an open and close time. [pause] Drivers arrive, wait if they're early, do the delivery, move on. ..... Some orders are marked **express**. [slowly] Those take longer to deliver, and the customers on them pay close attention to whether we arrive on time. [pause][low voice] We hear about it when those are late.



### Scene 4 — Traffic and Westgate

[inhales] On traffic — mornings and evenings are heavy. [pause] Lunch hour too — everyone's out at the same time. [pause] And Westgate has roadworks going on, [sniffles] excuse me, and has for a while, ..... so anything in or out of there in the morning takes longer than it should. [pause][slowly] They keep saying it'll be finished.



### Scene 5 — Trucks and capacities

The trucks have different capacities — [firmly] some hold more, some hold less. [pause] We load them at the depot in the morning, [pause] so once a driver is out, [pause] that's what they have for the day. ..... [low] They can't come back to pick up another order.



### Scene 6 — What we don't love, and the shift cap

[inhales] We don't like trucks driving back and forth across town more than they need to — [pause] it eats up the day, ..... and longer routes mean drivers stuck on the road instead of getting through their orders. [pause]

[pause] Shift hours — eight is the limit. [pause] Pay rules, fatigue rules, [low] we've had issues before.



### Scene 7 — The drivers

[pause][clears throat] About the drivers... [pause]

Alice [pause] she's been with us a while. She'll do most routes, but she doesn't like Westgate — says the roadworks make it slow and frustrating. [pause]

Carol is reliable, [pause] but she gets stressed when her truck has mostly express stops [slowly] one after another.

Dave [pause] he tells us himself — [slowly] after about six and a half hours, he's not as focused as he was at hour two. [pause] We try to listen.

Bob and Eve — no specific concerns.



### Scene 8 — How drivers see the day

[inhales] One more thing — drivers talk to each other. [pause] If one has been working routes all day [pause] and another got a short four-hour day, ..... [low voice] we hear about it the next morning.



### Scene 9 — No drops, occasional locked orders

[pause] Oh — every order has to be completed. [pause] [firmly] No skipping one because it's inconvenient — the customer paid, we deliver. ..... [pause] And occasionally a particular order has to go with a particular driver — the customer asked, ..... or there's a relationship. [pause] When that comes up, [firmly] we keep that assignment. Don't change it.



### Scene 10 — Hand-off

[inhales] All the details — orders, drivers, time windows, traffic — are in your printout. ..... [pause] We've also saved them as data files you can upload to the AI tool. [pause]

[low][slowly] So [pause] talk to it, ..... work through what matters most to us, ..... and see if it can put together a schedule we'd actually use. [pause] There's no one right answer — [pause] we're curious how you'd approach it [sniffles].

<break time='1s'/>

If something doesn't make sense, ask the AI first — it can answer most things. [pause] For anything else, [pause] please ask the researcher.

<break time='1s'/>

[quietly] Alright [loud click]
