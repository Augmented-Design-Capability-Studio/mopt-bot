# QuickBite Briefing Video — Script

A ~3–4 minute video voiced by **Mollie**, the QuickBite delivery manager. Played at the **Task briefing** step (`STUDY_DETAILED_PLAN.md` §5, step 5). Followed by participant reading the printed reference document (`PHYSICAL_REFERENCE.md`).

## Editor note (not for participants)

This script deliberately avoids optimization vocabulary (no "constraints", "soft / hard", "objectives", "trade-offs", "stochastic", "violations") and does **not** enumerate the cost-function components. Mollie speaks in colloquial dispatcher terms — operational concerns, fleet anecdotes, driver personalities — leaving the participant to translate her concerns into goals, priorities, and rules in their own language when speaking with the AI. See `STUDY_DETAILED_PLAN.md` §1 (*Observe operational-to-formal translation*) and §5 step 5 for why this matters. **Future edits:** if you find yourself adding language like "we want to balance workload" or "this is a soft preference", soften it back into operational/anecdotal phrasing — that translation is what we're studying.

## TTS conventions

- `[inhales]`, `[sniffles]`, `[clears throat]`, `[loud click]` etc. — voice ticks
- `[pause]`, `[long pause]` — in-line timing
- `<break time='Ns'/>` — SSML break between paragraphs
- `[slowly]`, `[firmly]`, `[low]`, `[energetically]`, `[lightly]`, `[whispering]` — delivery direction
- `### Scene N` markdown headers below are **for the recorder / director only** and are not narrated.

## Voice direction

A 35-year-old midwestern blue-collar woman with a low-to-medium-pitched voice. She sits next to a food delivery loading zone. Her voice is firm and relatively slow but a bit rushed; speech pattern is unstable. Audio quality similar to a smartphone voice recorder.

### Scene 3 — Time windows and express

### Scene 5 — Trucks and capacities

The trucks all have different capacities — [firmly] some hold more, some hold less. [pause] We load everything up at the depot in the morning, [pause] so once a driver's out on the route, [pause] that's what they've got. ..... [low] No coming back to grab another order. ### Scene 6 — What we don't love, and the shift cap

[pause] Same with shift hours — [pause] eight hours is the cap. [pause] Pay rules, fatigue rules, [low] we've had issues before.
